## What is Tadasana (Mountain Pose)?

Tadasana is the foundational standing pose in yoga. It teaches proper alignment and body awareness, serving as the blueprint for all other standing poses.

## Benefits

- Improves posture and body awareness
- Strengthens thighs, knees, and ankles
- Firms abdomen and buttocks
- Relieves sciatica
- Reduces flat feet
- Develops mental focus and concentration

## 20-Point Validation Checklist

### Foundation (Feet & Ankles)
- [ ] Feet hip-width apart
- [ ] Weight evenly distributed on both feet
- [ ] Feet parallel to each other
- [ ] All four corners of feet grounded

### Legs
- [ ] Quadriceps engaged (thigh muscles active)
- [ ] Knees straight but not locked (165-190¬∞)
- [ ] Knees tracking over ankles
- [ ] Inner thighs slightly rotating inward

### Pelvis & Core
- [ ] Hips level (not tilted)
- [ ] Pelvis neutral (not tucked or arched)
- [ ] Core gently engaged
- [ ] Tailbone lengthening down

### Spine & Torso
- [ ] Spine elongated
- [ ] Shoulders directly over hips
- [ ] Chest open and lifted
- [ ] Rib cage not jutting forward

### Shoulders & Arms
- [ ] Shoulders level and relaxed
- [ ] Shoulder blades sliding down back
- [ ] Collarbones broad
- [ ] Arms hanging naturally by sides
- [ ] Fingers active and energized

### Head & Neck
- [ ] Head aligned over shoulders
- [ ] Neck long, crown reaching up
- [ ] Chin parallel to floor
- [ ] Jaw relaxed

## Common Mistakes & Fixes

### Mistake 1: Feet Too Wide or Too Narrow
**Fix**: Measure hip-width by placing fists between ankles

### Mistake 2: Locked Knees
**Fix**: Micro-bend knees, engage thighs to support

### Mistake 3: Shoulders Hunched
**Fix**: Roll shoulders back and down, broaden collarbones

### Mistake 4: Head Forward
**Fix**: Imagine string pulling crown upward, chin slightly tucked

### Mistake 5: Arched Lower Back
**Fix**: Engage core, lengthen tailbone down

### Mistake 6: Weight on Heels or Toes
**Fix**: Rock forward/back to find center, ground all four corners

## Training Progression

### Week 1: Foundation
- **Goal**: Achieve 60%+ score consistently
- **Focus**: Foot placement and weight distribution
- **Duration**: 30 seconds holds

### Week 2: Alignment
- **Goal**: Achieve 70%+ score
- **Focus**: Spinal alignment and shoulder position
- **Duration**: 1 minute holds

### Week 3: Refinement
- **Goal**: Achieve 80%+ score
- **Focus**: Fine-tuning all points
- **Duration**: 2 minute holds

### Week 4: Mastery
- **Goal**: Achieve 90%+ score
- **Focus**: Maintaining perfect form with breath awareness
- **Duration**: 3+ minute holds

## Breathing in Tadasana

1. **Inhale**: Feel the breath lift your chest and lengthen your spine
2. **Exhale**: Root down through your feet, engage your core
3. **Rhythm**: 4 counts in, 4 counts out
4. **Focus**: Maintain alignment while breathing naturally

## Advanced Variations (After Mastery)

Once you consistently score 90%+, try:

1. **Eyes Closed**: Test proprioception and balance
2. **Arms Overhead**: Extend arms up, palms facing
3. **On Toes**: Rise onto balls of feet
4. **One Leg**: Lift one foot slightly off ground

## Tips for Better Detection

1. **Lighting**: Face a window or light source
2. **Background**: Plain, uncluttered background works best
3. **Clothing**: Fitted clothes help keypoint detection
4. **Camera**: Keep camera stable, not handheld
5. **Full Body**: Ensure head to feet are visible

## When to Practice

- **Morning**: Energize your day, set intentions
- **Midday**: Reset posture after sitting
- **Evening**: Release tension, ground yourself
- **Before Yoga**: Warm up and center

## Integration with Full Practice

Use Tadasana as:
- **Starting Point**: Begin every practice here
- **Reset Pose**: Return between standing poses
- **Alignment Check**: Reference for all standing poses
- **Meditation**: Stand in Tadasana for standing meditation

## Safety Notes

- If you have high or low blood pressure, don't hold too long
- Pregnant practitioners: widen stance for stability
- Knee issues: keep micro-bend, don't lock
- Balance problems: practice near wall for support

## Mindfulness in Tadasana

While holding the pose:
- Feel the connection between feet and earth
- Notice the subtle adjustments your body makes
- Observe your breath without changing it
- Cultivate stillness in body and mind
